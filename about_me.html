A brief recap of
my professional journey

Born and raised in Patagonia, I grew up very close to nature which I believe seed a passion for the world around me. I recall being very curious since an early age, and thankfully I carried that to eventually pursue a carrier in Physics. Along my journey I not only fulfilled that hunger for knowledge but also I found myself in a never ending playground. It is exciting to tackle a new problem, to plan the project and it is a thrill to dive into the struggle; where we put ourselves, our models, our methods and our tools to a test. But perhaps what is more important is that at the end of the task, the team has grown in understanding and we can provide real, tangible value to our clients or colleagues.
I was quite wise to follow great mentors that came along, many professors that not only shared their time and knowledge but that taught me great values for my professional life, I am talking dedication, commitment, honor to the schedules, fellowship and perseverance. But perhaps the most fundamental and useful tool that I got to practice through all my university years was the art of team-working. There is no science without that ingredient nor fun at all. So those elements are not only always strongly present in my mind, but I believe they drove me, along with my friends and colleagues to face our first projects.
I think there are two very different projects, that without going to much into detail, showcase some of my key personal attributes. Both of them were carried out my last two years of Master, but the first was ... Vicksek Simulation. The second one was a BusLine-Layout Optimization for my city's bus system.
