Synthetic Sample Simulator Engine

In-house engine (python-based) to synthesize signal samples from industrial machines.

Feb 2024 - Present Santiago Noacco

A brief introduction ...

This project encompasses the development & deployment of a Simulator Engine, encapsulated in a Python package. The goal of the Simulator is to generate synthetic samples that resemble the signatures measured by Tractian sensors, for as many different types of assets in as many possible operating scenarios, health conditions and external constrains.

Having an easy-to-use, trustful, feature-rich, extensible and very flexible Tool capable of producing labeled (synthetic) datasets, opens a world of opportunities for Data Scientist & Machine Learning Engineering Teams. The operating conditions of some assets is so unusual (yet crucial) that it is very hard to collect data to train ML models, however with a Tool like this one it is easy to overcome this issue. Among other use-cases, it enables to build speed up development since it reduces the effort & time taken to find a training data-set, and a common data-set can be reused to define performance baselines. It can create data-sets for testing environments and to build virtual experiments.

This project faced me with great challenges, both technical and of coordination. As the project leader with the support and availability of other 7 Data Scientist, I structured the development & deliverable cycle into 3 initial phases. In the first stage, which took about a month, consisted on drafting the package (tool) structure, validating the approach, creating the beta-release for testing & feedback assessment, and setting the success metrics and goals of the tool. The second stage was aimed to increase the power, coverage and flexibility of the tool. For that purpose it was necessary to onboard 4 of the Data Scientist into the development cycle of the package. With their contributions, added fixes & improvements we were able to release version 1 in record time, just over a month. The third phase has a period of consolidation, feedback collection and market spread. We are continuously increasing the power of the tool as the features are required.


A deep overview of the Simulator

Technically the Tool has two components: a collection of Seeds, and the Engine that synthesizes the samples from the Seeds.
